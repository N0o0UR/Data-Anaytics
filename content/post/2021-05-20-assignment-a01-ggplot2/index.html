The Effect of Vitamin C on Tooth Growth in Guinea Pigs

In this blog we are going to show the effect of providing vitamin C on the growth of the odontoblasts of 60 guinea pigs.

Keywords:

  • Odontoblasts: cells responsible for tooth growth
  • Ascorbic acid: a form of vitamin C and coded as VC.

Each pig received one of three dose levels of vitamin C which are:

  1. 0.5 mg/day
  2. 1 mg/day
  3. 2 mg/day

The Vitamin C dose is provided by one of the two following delivery methods:

  1. Through orange juice.
  2. Through ascorbic acid.

The collected data is composed of 60 observations one for each pig where the tooth length, taken dose and the delivery method are recorded.

Data and Visualisation

In this section we are going to analyse and visualize the relationship between the variables in our data set through using the data visualization package ggplot2

plotting the relationship between the tooth length and the given dose:

library(ggplot2)
ggplot(ToothGrowth, aes(len,dose,shape=supp,color=supp)) + 
  geom_point() +
  labs(title="                Tooth Length Vs Dose for each Delivery Method",x="Tooth Length",y="Dose")

From the above graph we can see that the orange juice delivery method results in better tooth growth with the 0.5 and 1 mg/day doses while ascorbic acid delivery method results in better tooth growth with 2 mg/day dose.

plotting the dose histogram along with the supplement type:

ggplot(ToothGrowth,aes(x=dose,fill=supp))+
  theme(axis.ticks.x=element_blank(), axis.text.x =element_blank() )+
  geom_bar()+
  labs(title="                Dose Histogram providing  the Delivery method",x="Dose")+
  facet_wrap(~ dose)

library(ggridges)
ggplot(ToothGrowth,aes(x=len,fill=supp))+
geom_density(alpha=0.5)+
  labs(title="                Tooth growth based on the Delivery method",x="Tooth Length",y="Density",fill="Delivery Method")

ggplot(ToothGrowth, aes(x = supp, y = len)) + geom_boxplot(aes(fill = supp))

ggplot(ToothGrowth,aes(x=dose,y=len,color=supp))+
